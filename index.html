About me

Hi! I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the University of Houston.

As a remote sensing data scientist, my interest focuses on using satellite altimetry, optical and radar (SAR) imagery, hydrological model, and machine learning

on flood forecasting. I have been working on a NASA-funded Project, called SERVIR, with study area over the Southeast Asia. The objective is to help data-sparse

developing countries to build sustainable and independent capacity for early flood risk assessment and proper effective decision making. I have developed flood water

level and spatial inundation forecasting systems. I have also delievered several training activities to the local stakeholders, for them to learn our techniques. I am

also working on a NOAA-funded Project, which I use optical imagery (VIIRS) to forecast areal water fraction in the U.S.
