About me

Hi! I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the University of Houston. As a remote sensing data scientist, my interest is to apply remotely sensed data

for disaster prevention, early risk assessment and to help the authorities to do meaningful decision making.


My current research focuses on using satellite altimetry, optical and radar (SAR) imagery, hydrological model, and machine learning technique to

perform flood forecasting. I have been working on a NASA-funded Project, called SERVIR, with study area over the Southeast Asia. The objective is

to help data-sparse developing countries build sustainable and independent capacity for early flood risk management. I have developed a flood water level

and a spatial inundation forecasting systems. I have also delievered several training activities to the local stakeholders, for them to learn the techniques.

I am also working on a NOAA-funded Project, which I use optical imagery (VIIRS) to forecast areal water fraction in the U.S.
